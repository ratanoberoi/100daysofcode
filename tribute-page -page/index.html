Sant Jarnail Singh Bhindranwale

The Gallant Defender

Here's a time line of Bhindranwale's life:

  • 1947 - born as Jarnail Singh Brar to a Jat Sikh family in 1947 in the village of Rode
  • 1953 - Leaves his family's farm to attend the University of Minnesota, thanks to a Depression era program known as the "National Youth Administration"
  • 1935 - He was put into a school in 1953 at the age of 6 but he dropped out of school five years later to work with his father on the farm.
  • 1971 - He married Pritam Kaur, the daughter of Sucha Singh of Bilaspur at the age of nineteen. The couple had two sons, Ishar Singh and Inderjit Singh, in 1971 and 1975, respectively.
  • 1965 - In 1965, he was enrolled by his father at the Damdami Taksal also known as Bhindran Taksal, a religious school, near Moga, Punjab, then headed by Gurbachan Singh Khalsa Bhindranwale.
  • 1978 - From his rise in popularity in 1978, until 6 June 1984 when Bhindranwale died, Bhindranwale propagated and practised hate thorough the orthodox Sikh tenets for six years.

"To many, Bhindranwale is remembered as a martyr to a movement which stood up to Indian state dominance.."

-- Ratan Singh Oberoi
